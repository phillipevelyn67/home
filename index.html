Bees 🐝

  • Honey bees can fly up to 20 miles per hour and visit up to 50 flowers per foraging trip.
  • A single bee colony can produce up to 60 pounds of honey per year.
  • Bees communicate with each other through a waggle dance to share information about food sources.

Birds 🐦

  • The Arctic Tern has the longest migration of any bird, traveling roughly 44,000 miles annually between the Arctic and Antarctic.
  • Hummingbirds are the only birds that can fly backwards, and their wings beat up to 80 times per second.
  • Peregrine Falcons are the fastest animals on Earth, reaching speeds over 240 miles per hour during their hunting dives.
  • Owls can rotate their heads up to 270 degrees thanks to their unique neck vertebrae.
  • A group of flamingos is called a "flamboyance" and they get their pink color from the food they eat.
